{# ── Section 1: Getting jobs into the system ────────────────────── #}

Getting jobs into the system

Google Sheet (recommended)

Paste a job URL into the "To Evaluate" tab of the recruiting Sheet. The scheduler runs every 6 hours (midnight, 6am, noon, 6pm UTC) and automatically fetches the JD, scores it, and adds it to the Dashboard. This is the primary inbound path for volume.

Score manually

Use the "Score a Job" form on the Dashboard — paste a URL or raw JD text. Scored immediately, no wait for the scheduler. Best for one-offs you want to evaluate right now.

{# ── Section 2: Understanding scores ──────────────────────────── #}

Understanding scores

Scores run 0–10 through four layers, in order:

Layer 1: Auto-Reject

Deterministic. Checks salary floor ($175k), blocked sectors (Healthcare, Gov, Gambling, Adult), and other hard filters. If triggered, final = 0.0 and the rest is skipped. No LLM calls fired.

Layer 2: Match to Candidate

Highest weight, ±3.0 range. One Gemini call compares the JD to the Accomplishments Inventory corpus. Returns a match score plus evidence rows, mismatches, tailored resume bullets, and cover-letter hooks. This is what the Match Analysis tab shows.

Layer 3: LLM Qualitative Adjustment

±1.0 range. Role-shape vibe check: IC vs. leadership balance, authority level, reporting line, firefighter signals.

Layer 4: Adjustment Weights

Additive, deterministic. Keyword signals: tech stack (Slack/Mac = +1, Teams/Windows = −2), greenfield language (+2), consumption pricing (+1), AI-native sector (+2), DevTools (+1.5), etc.

Formula: final = clamp(5.0 + Layer4 + Layer2 + Layer3, 0, 10)
Single clamp at the end.

{# ── Section 3: The Match Analysis tab ──────────────────────── #}

The Match Analysis tab

Only appears on Job detail pages (not Outreach Targets or Discovered). Only populated for jobs scored after May 17, 2026 (when Layer 2 was deployed).

What's shown
  • Match score — ±3.0 float with a 2-3 sentence fit summary
  • Evidence table — JD requirement → matched corpus accomplishment → Strong/Moderate/Weak
  • Mismatches — what the JD asks for that your corpus doesn't cover (your tailoring targets)
  • Tailored resume bullets — 3-5 pre-written bullets pulled from the Inventory for this specific role
  • Cover-letter hooks — 1-3 opening sentences anchored to corpus evidence
How to use tailored bullets
  1. Open the Match Analysis tab on the job detail page.
  2. Review the Evidence table — it shows which of your accomplishments maps to each JD requirement. If a match is Weak, that's a gap to address in your cover letter.
  3. Copy the Tailored Resume Bullets — swap these into the relevant experience section of your resume before submitting. They're written to match the exact language in this JD.
  4. Use one or two Cover Letter Hooks as your opening paragraph — they're anchored to corpus evidence and reference the company directly.
  5. For a print-ready resume with bullets pre-injected, click ⬇ Print Tailored Resume in the tab header, then Cmd+P → Save as PDF.
If a job shows "No summary recorded," it was scored before Layer 2 deployed. Go to Admin → Rescore All to backfill all missing Layer 2 data in one batch, or paste the original URL into the Dashboard "Score a Job" form to re-score a single job.
{# ── Section 4: Three areas of the app ────────────────────── #}

Three areas of the app

Inbound (jobs with a specific open role)
  • Dashboard — Score new jobs, see everything scored in the last 30 days
  • Discovered — Jobs auto-found by the discovery scanner from Hunt Target ATS feeds. Review and promote (→ Dashboard/Pipeline) or dismiss.
  • Pipeline — All jobs you've moved past Watchlist. Your active candidates.
  • Auto-rejected — Jobs that hit a hard filter. Review occasionally to confirm the filter is working as expected.
  • Ethics Vetting — Jobs that need your manual ethics sign-off before advancing.
Outbound (no open role yet — cold outreach)
  • Outreach Targets — Companies you want to cold-reach. Add them, run research, track outreach status. These are not scored jobs — no JD, no Layer 2.
  • Hunt Targets — Companies in your active discovery scan. The scanner checks their ATS for new openings and creates Discovered jobs automatically.
Interview & Pipeline tools
  • Interview Prep — Per-job interview hub: opening hook, question bank, cheat sheet generator, transcript analysis, interviewer research, coaching debrief.
  • Recruiters — Log recruiter contacts and track conversations.

Watchlist → Researching → Outreach → Applied → Screening → Interviewing → Offer → Accepted

Two exit paths: "I Declined" (you passed) and "They Declined" (they passed).

{# ── Section 6: Interview prep workflow ──────────────────── #}

Interview prep workflow

  1. Move a job to Researching — seeds the question bank with role-specific questions automatically. These are organized by persona (CFO, CRO, VP Eng, etc.) and priority.
  2. Open the job's detail page and click Interview prep → in the top-right action bar. This takes you to the dedicated Interview Prep page for that job (/prep/<job-id>).
  3. Before each round — generate a Cheat Sheet: scroll to the Cheat Sheet card, enter the round name (e.g. "HM Interview") and the interviewer name(s), then click Generate Cheat Sheet. Output is a one-pager with top likely questions, anchor story matches, talking points, and your opening hook. Print or save it as a PDF via Cmd+P before the call.
  4. Research your interviewer: scroll to Interviewer Research, enter their name and title, and click Research Interviewer. You get a background brief — likely concerns, communication style, and what they'll probably probe on.
  5. After each round — analyze the transcript: scroll to Analyze Transcript. Paste the full raw transcript in the first field (required — copy from Granola, Zoom, or any recorder). If Granola generated a summary or analysis, paste that in the second field (optional). Enter the interviewer's name and title, then click Analyze Transcript. Output includes answer scoring, filler word tracking, and a debrief with gaps to close in the next round.
  6. Drill specific answers: scroll to Interview Coaching, paste the transcript, and click Analyze Coaching. Gets per-answer feedback on any response you want to sharpen.
  7. Close out the round: check off questions as asked in the Question Bank at the bottom of the page. Unchecked questions roll forward to the next round automatically.
Divergence detection: if you ask the same question to multiple interviewers (CFO + CRO, for example), the app flags when their answers contradict — useful signal about org alignment before you decide to accept.
Transcript format: plain text is fine — paste directly from Granola's transcript view, Zoom's auto-transcript, or any recording tool. No special formatting required. Speaker labels ("Jeff:", "Sarah:") help the analysis but aren't required.
{# ── Section 6b: Notes ─────────────────────────────────── #}

Adding notes to a job

Every job has a free-form My Notes field at the top of its summary tab. Use it for anything that doesn't fit the structured fields: prep reminders, research tasks, concerns to track, or strategy notes.

Examples
  • "Applied 2026-06-01. Need to prep a case study on GitLab ARR growth before the HM screen."
  • "They require on-site 3 days/week — I'll probably be ruled out early. Try the relocation-flexibility angle."
  • "Check LinkedIn — the VP of CS joined 4 months ago, might be rebuilding the team."
Notes auto-save as you type (1.2s after you stop). A 📝 icon appears on dashboard and pipeline cards when a job has notes so you can spot them at a glance.
{# ── Section 7: Keeping the corpus current ─────────────── #}

Keeping the corpus current

The Match Analysis quality depends on the Accomplishments Inventory. Update it:

  • After every new major project, initiative, or metric you can cite
  • After any public talk, podcast, or published piece
  • When you have a new verified number to add

SSOT: ~/path/to/your/Accomplishments_Inventory.docx

Maintained in Cowork sessions. To deploy an update: 

cd ~/Projects/recruiting-engine
cp "/path/to/your/Accomplishments_Inventory.docx" data/Accomplishments_Inventory.docx
modal deploy app/main.py
git add data/Accomplishments_Inventory.docx && git commit -m "Update Inventory: [what changed]"
{# ── Section 8: Re-scoring existing jobs ────────────────── #}

Re-scoring existing jobs

Re-scoring overwrites the existing score with a fresh run of all four layers — useful after updating the Accomplishments Inventory or when you want Layer 2 data on an older job.

Batch re-score (all jobs missing Layer 2)

Go to Settings → Admin → Rescore All. This re-runs scoring on every job that has no Layer 2 (match_score IS NULL). Progress is shown inline. Rate-limited to avoid quota exhaustion — expect ~5 seconds per job.

Single job re-score

Paste the original JD URL into the "Score a Job" form on the Dashboard. The new score overwrites the old one — final score, Layer 2 data, tailored bullets, and cover-letter hooks all update.

Only Jobs (inbound pipeline) need re-scoring. Outreach Targets and Discovered jobs use a different scoring model and are unaffected.
